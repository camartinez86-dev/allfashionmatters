Adidas Gazelle Lo Pro: The Skinny Sneaker Dominating Spring 2026

Move over, Samba. There's a new thin-soled Adidas in town, and it's officially the season's most wanted sneaker.

Image: Adidas Gazelle Lo Pro Core Black (Photo via WWD)

For years, the Adidas Samba has reigned supreme as the It-sneaker of the fashion world. From street style icons to runway collaborations, the Samba has been virtually unavoidable. But as we step into Spring 2026, a new contender has emerged—and it's slimmer, sleeker, and more sophisticated than ever.

Enter the Adidas Gazelle Lo Pro: the thin-soled evolution of the classic Gazelle that's quickly becoming the must-have footwear of the season. Fashion girls across Paris, Milan, and New York have already made the switch, and here's why you should too.

What Makes the Gazelle Lo Pro Different?

Released in June 2025, the Gazelle Lo Pro represents Adidas's commitment to the "skinny sneaker" movement—a radical departure from the chunky dad sneakers that dominated the late 2010s and early 2020s. But what exactly sets this silhouette apart?

As WWD reports, "While the original Gazelle already featured a relatively slim sole, the Gazelle Lo Pro dials it down even further with a gum rubber bottom that's roughly half the size of the standard version."

But the changes don't stop at the sole. Adidas has completely reimagined the upper with:

  • A deconstructed build featuring a lower ankle opening
  • A stitched T-toe shape in place of the traditional overlay
  • Premium natural leather upper for elevated appeal
  • Textile lining for all-day comfort

The result? A shoe that maintains the general look of the classic Gazelle but in an even thinner—and arguably more fashionable—form.

The Rise of the "Skinny Sneaker" Trend

Image: Adidas Gazelle Lo Pro Sand Strata (Photo via WWD)

The Gazelle Lo Pro is part of a much larger fashion movement. As Glamour notes, "Ask any fashion insider what the silhouette of the moment is, and you'll get the same answer: the absolute thinnest soles possible, a look ushered in at the start of 2025 by Dries Van Noten."

This "skinny sneaker" trend represents a dramatic shift in fashion priorities. After years of embracing bulky, "ugly" sneakers (remember the Balenciaga Triple S?), the fashion world is now craving elegance, femininity, and a return to slim silhouettes.

German fashion magazine Grazia identifies the key driver: "A crucial reason for this hype lies in the return of ultra-flat soles, which in 2026 are finally replacing chunky dad sneakers."

Gazelle Lo Pro vs. Samba: What's the Difference?

You might be wondering: with both the Samba and Gazelle Lo Pro available, which should you choose? Here's the breakdown:

Feature Adidas Gazelle Lo Pro Adidas Samba
Sole Height Ultra-thin (half size) Low-profile
Aesthetic Elegant, feminine Sporty, retro
Price $110 $100

As Esquire Germany succinctly puts it: "While the Gazelle appears more sporty and functional, the Sambas are something more elegant and fashionable in everyday wear."

How to Style the Gazelle Lo Pro

The Parisian Girl

Pair your Gazelle Lo Pro with straight-leg jeans, a silk blouse, and a trench coat. The cream or sand shades complement neutral palettes perfectly.

Streetwear Edge

Go monochromatic with cargo pants, an oversized blazer, and the Core Black version for an elevated street look.

Spring Ready

The thin sole pairs beautifully with midi skirts, floral dresses, and linen trousers—perfect for transitional weather.

The Verdict

The Adidas Gazelle Lo Pro isn't just another sneaker release—it's a statement. In a world where fashion is oscillating between quiet luxury and bold expression, this shoe bridges the gap perfectly.

Whether you're team Samba or ready for something new, the Gazelle Lo Pro deserves a spot in your rotation. As the thin-soled trend continues to gain momentum throughout 2026, now is the time to invest.

At $110-120, it's also far more accessible than many luxury alternatives while still delivering that "expensive-looking" aesthetic that fashion insiders crave.
